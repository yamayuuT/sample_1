豚丼(rice covered with pork and vegetables)

内容物(contents)

炭火焼きぶた(charcoal-roasted pork)
細ネギ(scallion)
たれ(sauce for dipping, etc. made from soy sauce, mirin, vinegar and dashi)

　明治時代末ごろから十勝地方では養豚業がはじまり、豚肉が食べ親しまれてきた。養豚業が盛んであった十勝地方の帯広市が「豚丼」発祥の地といわれている。厚切りの豚肉を砂糖醤油で味付けしたタレでからめ、ごはんの上にのせた「豚丼」は帯広市の名物料理となっている。
昭和初期に帯広市内の食堂で、炭火焼きした豚肉にうなぎの蒲焼き風のタレを使用した丼をつくったのが「豚丼」の発祥といわれている。当初は、農家や開拓者が汗を流し働く姿を見て、スタミナ料理を提供したいと思い、食材にうなぎを使おうと考えたものの、うなぎは高価で手に入りにくかった。そこで目を付けたのが、豚肉だった。当時は、十勝地方では養豚業が盛んにおこなわれていたこともあって、豚肉は身近で手に入りやすかったという。こうして帯広の地で誕生した「豚丼」は、いまでは全国でも知られるほど有名となった。
主に豚肉はロースやバラ肉を使う。フライパンで豚肉を焼いた後、砂糖醤油のタレをからめていく。トッピングの具材は白髪ねぎが多いが、グリンピースなどをのせる場合がある。薬味以外の余計な具材が入っておらずいたってシンプルな料理。炭火で焼くとより一層香ばしくいただける。

Since the end of the Meiji Era (1868-1912), pig farming began in the Tokachi region, and pork has been a popular food in the region. Obihiro City in the Tokachi region, where pig farming was prosperous, is said to be the birthplace of "butadon" (pork bowl). Thick slices of pork, dipped in a sauce seasoned with sugar and soy sauce, and served on a bed of rice, "butadon" has become a specialty of Obihiro City.

It is said that the origin of "buta-don" dates back to the early Showa period, when restaurants in Obihiro City made a bowl of rice topped with charbroiled pork and eel kabayaki-style sauce. Initially, the city wanted to offer a stamina dish after seeing farmers and pioneers working hard, and thought of using eels as an ingredient, but eels were expensive and hard to come by. They decided to use pork as an ingredient, but eels were too expensive and difficult to obtain. At the time, pig farming was thriving in the Tokachi region, and pork was readily available. Thus, the "buta-don" (pork bowl) that was born in Obihiro became so famous that it is now known throughout Japan.

Pork loin or belly is mainly used. After grilling the pork in a frying pan, the pork is dipped in a sauce of sugar and soy sauce. The most common topping is white onion, but green peas and other ingredients may also be added. It is a very simple dish with no extra ingredients other than yakumi (condiments).Grilled over a charcoal fire, it is even more savory.
